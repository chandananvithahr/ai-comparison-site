## Introduction

Both **Captions AI** and **Opus Clip** claim to automate short-form video creation. But they approach the problem differently: Captions AI focuses on live recording and captioning, while Opus Clip focuses on repurposing long-form content into viral clips.

## What is Captions AI?

Captions AI is a mobile-first app that auto-captions your videos, corrects eye contact with AI, removes filler words, and adds animated subtitles. It's built for creators who record themselves.

Key features:
- Best-in-class auto-caption accuracy
- AI eye contact correction (huge for talking-head videos)
- Filler word removal
- Animated caption styles
- Starting at $7/month

## What is Opus Clip?

Opus Clip takes your long YouTube videos, podcasts, or webinars and automatically identifies the most engaging moments — then clips, captions, and formats them for TikTok, Shorts, and Reels.

Key features:
- AI "virality score" for each clip
- Auto-captions + animated text
- Auto-generated b-roll
- One-click multi-platform publishing
- 60 free minutes/month
- Starting at $19/month

## Key Difference: Recording vs. Repurposing

This is the core distinction:
- **Captions AI:** You record → it polishes
- **Opus Clip:** You have long content → it finds clips

If you're a podcaster or YouTuber with hours of existing content, Opus Clip is the obvious choice. If you're a TikToker recording 60-second pieces to camera, Captions AI is perfect.

## Quality Comparison

**Captions AI** produces the best-looking auto-captions on the market. Its eye contact correction is genuinely impressive — it shifts your pupils to face the camera even when you're reading from a teleprompter.

**Opus Clip's** automatic clip selection is hit or miss. It scores clips on a "virality scale," but the algorithm sometimes misses the most compelling moments. The output requires manual review and cleanup.

## Pricing

**Captions AI:**
- Free: basic captions
- Pro: $7/month
- Max: $20/month

**Opus Clip:**
- Free: 60 min/month
- Starter: $19/month
- Pro: $49/month

Captions AI is significantly cheaper. Opus Clip costs more but serves a different, higher-value use case.

## Who Should Use Captions AI?

- TikTok/Instagram creators recording talking-head videos
- Creators who want the cleanest auto-captions
- Anyone with eye contact issues on camera
- Budget-conscious creators ($7/mo vs $19/mo)

## Who Should Use Opus Clip?

- Podcasters and YouTubers with long-form content
- Marketers repurposing webinars or interviews
- Creators who want automation with minimal manual editing
- Anyone publishing to multiple platforms simultaneously

## Verdict: Use Both, But For Different Reasons

These tools aren't really competitors — they're complementary. But if forced to choose one:

- **Short-form first creators:** Captions AI
- **Long-form repurposers:** Opus Clip

> **Bottom line:** Podcaster or YouTuber? Get Opus Clip. Recording talking-head content? Get Captions AI. Running both channels? Use both — they barely overlap.
