## Two Different Approaches to AI Editing

**Descript** and **Opus Clip** are both AI-powered video editing tools, but the comparison is a bit like comparing a Swiss Army knife to a scalpel. Descript is a full-featured editing platform. Opus Clip does one thing and aims to do it extremely well: automatically turning long-form video into short-form viral clips.

Understanding this fundamental difference is the key to choosing between them.

## Descript vs Opus Clip at a Glance

- **Descript** = Edit long-form video and audio by editing a transcript. Great for podcasts, interviews, tutorials, and full YouTube videos.
- **Opus Clip** = Feed in a long video, get back a set of short clips optimized for TikTok, Reels, and Shorts. Great for repurposing existing content.

Many creators actually use **both** — Descript to produce and polish the long-form content, then Opus Clip to extract short clips for social distribution.

## Deep Dive: Descript

Descript's core innovation is treating video and audio as text. When you import your recording, Descript transcribes it automatically. Then, to cut out a rambling tangent, you simply delete those sentences from the transcript — and the video is cut accordingly.

This workflow is transformative for podcast editors, interview video editors, and anyone who spends hours scrubbing timelines to find specific moments. Descript makes the whole process feel more like editing a Google Doc.

**Standout Descript features:**
- Filler word removal ("um", "uh", "like") with a single click
- Overdub: clone your voice to fix flubs without re-recording
- Screen recording built-in
- Automatic audiogram creation for social
- Multi-track audio mixing
- Collaboration with team comments and approval workflows

**Where Descript struggles:**
- Export quality has occasional inconsistencies (occasional audio sync issues on complex projects)
- The transcript-based editing paradigm takes time to learn
- Not designed for generative AI video creation
- Can feel slow on large projects

## Deep Dive: Opus Clip

Opus Clip takes a fundamentally different approach. You don't edit video with Opus Clip — you repurpose it. Feed the tool a YouTube video, a podcast recording, a webinar, or any long-form content, and its AI identifies the most compelling 60-90 second moments, creates clips, adds captions, and even generates a viral score predicting how well each clip will perform.

The AI is genuinely impressive at finding hooks, emotional peaks, and key insights. It also automatically:
- Adds dynamic animated captions
- Crops to vertical (9:16) format
- Adds b-roll overlays
- Identifies the best speaker to focus on in multi-person recordings

**Where Opus Clip struggles:**
- The AI sometimes misses context and selects clips that lack narrative coherence out of context
- Limited manual editing — if the AI's clip choices aren't right, fixing them is cumbersome
- Relies heavily on the assumption that automation will get it right
- Not suitable for original long-form video creation

## Pricing Showdown

**Descript:**
- Free: 1 hour transcription, basic features
- Hobbyist: $24/month (10 hours/month transcription)
- Creator: $40/month (30 hours/month)
- Business: $80/month (100 hours/month)

**Opus Clip:**
- Free: 60 minutes of upload per month, watermarked exports
- Starter: $19/month (150 mins upload, no watermark)
- Pro: $49/month (600 mins upload, team access)
- Business: Custom

Opus Clip is cheaper at entry level, but the minute-based upload limits can become restrictive quickly if you're repurposing a lot of content. Descript's pricing scales better for heavy users.

## Who Should Use Descript?

Descript is ideal for:
- **Podcast producers** — the text-based workflow is perfect for interview-style audio editing
- **YouTubers creating tutorials or vlogs** — edit efficiently without timeline scrubbing
- **Course creators** — polish recordings and fix mistakes without re-recording
- **Teams** — collaborative editing and approval workflows are strong
- **Anyone who wants Overdub** — voice cloning for corrections is genuinely magic

## Who Should Use Opus Clip?

Opus Clip is ideal for:
- **YouTubers with existing back catalog** — instantly repurpose hundreds of videos for Shorts
- **Podcast hosts** — turn episodes into audiogram-style TikTok clips automatically
- **Social media managers** — feed in conference talks, webinars, or events and get clips for LinkedIn and Instagram
- **Creators who hate editing** — when you want clips but don't want to manually cut them yourself

## The Verdict

**Descript** is the overall winner for versatility and power. Its text-based editing approach is genuinely revolutionary and makes it an invaluable tool for any creator producing long-form audio or video content.

**Opus Clip** is not a replacement for Descript — it's a complement. For creators who already have a library of long-form content and want to distribute it across short-form channels without spending hours editing, Opus Clip is exceptional at what it does.

> **Recommendation:** If you create original long-form content, start with Descript. If you already have a catalog of content and want to repurpose it for social media, Opus Clip will save you dozens of hours per month.
